# The Beckmann Rearrangement:

## Introduction

The Beckmann rearrangement named after German Ernst Otto Beckmann is a classic acid-mediated transformation converting oxime into amides. The reaction has significant industrial importance such as the production of Nylon-6 or synethesis of paracetamol drug *(Hoechst-Celanese)*.

The reaction provides a key example in the **class of rearrangement reaction involving intramolecular migration with stereochemical control**.

<img
  src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/eb/BeckmannRearrangement3.svg"
  alt="Beckmann Reaction Mechanism"

/>

## Reaction Mechanism

<img
  src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8c/Mechanism_Beckmann_rearrangement.svg"
  alt="Beckmann Reaction Mechanism"
  width="80%"
/>

The reaction proceds by protonation of the -OH of oxime via an acid *(protic or Lewis)*. The water moleculue leaves and simultanaouesly the alkyl group
anti to -H2O+ migrates forming the nitrilium ion which then undergoes solvolysis *(can be attacked by another nucleophile)*. The product attains an imide-amide tautomerization equilibrium.

A natural and interesting question arises as to which group can be found by studying the steric and electronic requirements which we might discuss in a future post. Simply 
and safely speaking it's always the group anti to -OH which migrates *(happens to be the bulky group)*. However, one must take note of the fact that with **protic acids promote significant oxime geometrical isomerization** is possible and thus the group more abled to donate electron density is seen to migrate. 

<img
  src="/public/images/beckmann/isomerization.png"
  alt="Beckmann Isomerization Mechanism"
  width="50%"
/>

## Competitive Reaction: The Beckmann Fragmentation

If the migrating group can exist as a stable carbocation intermediate there is an increased probability of the migrating group not migrating *(intra)*, but rather leaving as a carbocation intermediate *(fragmentation)*.
<img
  src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a2/BeckmannFragmentationKirihara1997.svg"
  alt="Beckmann Fragmentation Mechanism"
  width="50%"
/>

The fragmentation pathway creates a nitrile and carbocation intermediate.

<img
  src="/public/images/beckmann/beckmann_fragmentation.png"
  alt="Beckmann Fragmentation Mechanism"
  width="50%"
/>
